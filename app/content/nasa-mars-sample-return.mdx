# NASA Delays Mars Sample Return Decision to 2026

NASA has deferred its final decision on the Mars Sample Return architecture until mid-2026, aiming for a reduced timeline and cost.

#### Focusing Facts

- The project's estimated cost has risen from $3 billion to between $8 billion and $11 billion.
- NASA's two options include using a 'sky crane' or collaborating with private companies.
- China aims to return its Mars samples by 2031.

#### Context

NASA's delay in deciding the Mars Sample Return mission reflects historical budget overruns similar to Apollo's 1969 moon landing, which was achieved despite cost challenges. Comparison with ESA's Rosetta mission in 2004 highlights potential for successful collaboration despite setbacks. The project is pivotal, echoing the Voyager 1977 mission's importance for interplanetary exploration. It endangers U.S. leadership in space exploration amidst China's competitive space advancements. If canceled, it mirrors the 1993 Space Exploration Initiative downfall, potentially pushing U.S. planetary science into secondary importance globally.
