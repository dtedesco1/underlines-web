# Fed Signals Slower Rate Cuts Amid Trump Policy Uncertainty

Federal Reserve signals slower interest rate cuts due to inflation risks from Trump's trade and immigration policies.

#### Focusing Facts

- Federal Reserve cut benchmark rate to 4.25%-4.5% in December.

- Fed officials anticipate only two rate cuts in 2025, down from four previously.

- Trump considers invoking emergency powers for tariffs, stirring inflation concerns.

#### Context

This scenario echoes past interactions between the White House and Federal Reserve, such as Nixon's wage and price controls in 1971 and Trump's own tariff strategy during his first term in 2018. Historically, White House pressure has occasionally swayed Fed actions, reminiscent of when LBJ influenced Fed decisions in the late 1960s. Currently, the Fed aims to maintain independence amidst political tension reminiscent of its stance during the Obama administration's post-2008 era of economic stimulus. This moment marks a potential pivot in monetary policy, reflecting broader global trends of increased inflation risks due to aggressive national policies. Similar past events, such as the Volcker Shock of the 1970s, suggest that if inflation spikes, a more hawkish Fed stance might be inevitable, possibly leading to tighter credit conditions globally. However, the unique combination of trade policy and executive pressure adds a complex layer that could lead to prolonged uncertainty and market volatility, reminiscent of the 2013 taper tantrum.
