# Trump’s Aggressive Tariffs Spark Global Trade Turmoil and Fed Dilemma

On April 3-4, President Trump unleashed unexpectedly steep tariffs, overwhelming market expectations and forcing Fed Chair Powell to warn of a higher-than-expected inflation surge and slowed growth.

#### Focusing Facts

- Tariffs, announced with a 10% baseline and potentially reaching an average of 27%, were met with retaliatory measures like China’s 34% tariff on U.S. goods.

- Global markets reacted sharply, with the S&P 500 dropping about 3% and prospects for rate cuts delayed amid forecasts of an inflation boost of up to 1.5 percentage points.

- Diverse forecasts now range from zero to multiple Fed rate cuts, highlighting uncertainty over whether the inflationary impact will be transitory or persistent.

#### Context

This event recalls the Smoot-Hawley Tariff of 1930, which intensified global trade tensions and deepened economic downturns, illustrating the long-term risks of protectionist policies. Instead of the gradual adjustments seen after crises like the 2007-2009 financial downturn, Trump’s sudden, steep tariffs have thrown markets into uncertainty and forced the Fed into a tighter policy corner. Over the next century, such aggressive trade interventions can recalibrate how central banks balance growth with price stability, potentially leading to prolonged periods of economic sluggishness and entrenched inflation if not reversed. The historical echoes highlight the enduring impact of policy shocks that reshape global economic systems, underscoring the importance of measured approaches in an increasingly interconnected world.
