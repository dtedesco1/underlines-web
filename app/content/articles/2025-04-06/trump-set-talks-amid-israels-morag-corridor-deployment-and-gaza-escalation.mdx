# Trump-Set Talks Amid Israel’s ‘Morag Corridor’ Deployment and Gaza Escalation

On April 6, 2025, President Trump is scheduled for his second meeting with Prime Minister Netanyahu amid Israel’s controversial establishment of the 'Morag Corridor' in southern Gaza and escalating military operations targeting Hamas.

#### Focusing Facts

- Israel deployed troops of its 36th Division to establish the 'Morag Corridor', cutting off Rafah in southern Gaza on April 6, 2025.

- Trump and Netanyahu confirmed a White House meeting on Monday, April 6, 2025, to discuss Gaza hostages, a 17% tariff, and broader Middle Eastern issues.

- In a prior February meeting, Trump proposed resettling displaced Palestinians outside Gaza—a suggestion that drew severe international criticism.

#### Context

This event echoes historical instances of high-stakes diplomatic maneuvers in volatile regions, reminiscent of the Cold War’s brinkmanship moments like the Cuban Missile Crisis (1962) where strategic corridors and deterrence played critical roles. The current developments in Gaza, marked by infrastructure gains such as the 'Morag Corridor' and unprecedented diplomatic engagements, are part of long-term systemic shifts in Middle Eastern power balances and US-Israel relations. Over the next century, the integration of military strategies, economic pressures like tariffs, and controversial humanitarian policies could redefine state behavior and international law norms, reinforcing the cyclical nature of conflicts rooted in disputed sovereignties and alliance politics.
