# Trump’s Pivot Spurs Ceasefire Talks Tied to Sanctions Relief for Russia

In early March 2025, Trump's administration shifted its policy with sweeping concessions that enabled conditional ceasefire negotiations in the Black Sea and opened doorways to lifting key sanctions on Russia.

#### Focusing Facts

- US Cyber Command halted offensive planning against Russia during peace talks, and negotiators paused collaboration with counter-disinformation units.

- Russian and Ukrainian delegations in Saudi Arabia agreed in principle to a 30-day ceasefire for Black Sea navigation, contingent on lifting sanctions on banks like Rosselkhozbank.

- Trump’s team proposed economic incentives including restored access to SWIFT and eased maritime restrictions as part of broader concessions.

#### Context

This episode recalls the détente strategies of the 1970s, such as Nixon’s 1972 Moscow visit that realigned US-Soviet relations, though with far more complex modern stakes. The current maneuvering over ceasefire agreements and sanctions relief reflects long-running trends of shifting power dynamics and redefined alliances, undercutting post-World War II security frameworks. Over a century, the blending of economic incentives with military negotiations has periodically upended established orders; however, whether this loose alignment will yield lasting stability or merely postpone further conflict remains deeply uncertain in the evolving multipolar world.
