# Assam Cabinet Greenlights Rs 1.22 Lakh Cr Investment at Advantage Assam 2.0 Summit

Assam's Cabinet meticulously approved Rs 1.22 lakh crore in vetted MoU proposals ahead of the Feb 25-26 summit, while rejecting proposals deemed unsound.

#### Focusing Facts

- Approved MoUs total Rs 1.22 lakh crore alongside 2,600 district-level agreements worth Rs 15,900 crore.

- Cabinet rejected proposals valued between Rs 35,000-45,000 crore on flimsy grounds.

- Infosys launched a 230-member Project Development Centre and Australia revised its travel advisory for Assam, excluding four districts.

#### Context

Historically, regional investment summits have served as catalysts for economic transformation in India—much like Gujarat’s early 2000s initiatives that spurred rigorous growth—yet Assam's approach stands out by pre-scrutinizing investment proposals before signing MoUs. This measured governance echoes lessons from post-1991 liberalization, aiming to prevent over-promising and ensure sustainable long-term development. Coupled with strategic tech advancements such as Infosys' new centre and efforts to improve international perceptions (illustrated by the eased travel advisory), this moment signals Assam’s evolving role in rebalancing regional development and setting a cautious precedent for investment that could shape its trajectory over the next century.
