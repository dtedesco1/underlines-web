# NASA Slashes 2024 YR4 Impact Odds to 1 in 360 After New Data

NASA’s latest measurements have revised the impact probability for asteroid 2024 YR4 from a peak of 3.1% on Feb 18 to just 0.28% on Feb 21, indicating a far lower risk for a potential 2032 encounter.

#### Focusing Facts

- Impact odds dropped from 3.1% (1 in 32) to 0.28% (1 in 360) after overnight observations between Feb 19-21.

- The asteroid, approximately 55 meters wide and capable of releasing about 8 megatons of energy, was downgraded from Torino level 3 to level 1.

- International teams, including Chile’s ATLAS and Australian space experts, contributed to refining its orbital path.

#### Context

This sequence of events is reminiscent of past near-miss recalibrations such as the 2004 Apophis episode, where initial alarm gave way to refined safety assessments as measurement techniques improved. Over recent decades, as our detection technologies and global coordination in planetary defense have advanced, similar transient risk upticks have been common. This iterative process, stretching back to the early days of space observation in the 1960s, illustrates not only the inherent uncertainties in predicting celestial orbits but also the steady enhancement of our capacity to monitor and mitigate potential impacts—an evolution that, over a 100-year horizon, has greatly bolstered planetary defense strategies.
