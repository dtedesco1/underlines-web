# Paris Summit: European Leaders Reject Russian Conditions, Boost Ukraine Support

On March 27, 2025, over 30 national leaders convened in Paris to reinforce military and economic backing for Ukraine amidst a US-led ceasefire negotiation, dismissing Russian demands.

#### Focusing Facts

- The Paris summit was attended by leaders from over 30 nations, including French President Macron, Ukrainian President Zelensky, and UK’s Prime Minister Starmer.

- France announced an additional €2 billion in military aid to Ukraine, incorporating anti-tank missiles, surface-to-air systems, armored vehicles, drones, and fighter jets.

- Discussions also featured proposals for a non-combat European 'reassurance force' in Ukraine as part of a future peace settlement.

#### Context

This summit resembles pivotal historical moments such as the post-WWII reordering of European security in 1949 with NATO’s formation, when European nations sought greater strategic autonomy in the face of external threats. In 2025, the coalition’s effort to enhance Ukraine's defense while rejecting Russian conditions reflects long-term trends toward European self-reliance and coordinated security guarantees. As was the case in mid-20th century Europe, these measures signal a deliberate move away from reliance on external powers, aiming to reshape regional stability and deterrence strategies over the next century, even as current negotiations reveal the complexities of balancing divided global alliances.
