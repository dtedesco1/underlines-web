# Northeast’s Double Sunrise: Deep Partial Solar Eclipse on March 29, 2025

On March 29, 2025, a deep partial solar eclipse transforms sunrise in Northeast U.S. and eastern Canada into a rare spectacle featuring a split, 'double sunrise' effect.

#### Focusing Facts

- At Quoddy Head State Park in Maine, observers will witness an eclipse covering approximately 83% to 87% of the sun.

- Northern Quebec near Akulivik will experience up to 93% obscuration, while select locations display a unique dual cusp phenomenon.

- The eclipse spans from 4:50 a.m. to 8:43 a.m. EDT, varying regionally with peak timings from roughly 6:42 to 7:08 a.m.

#### Context

This March 29 event echoes the dramatic total solar eclipse of April 2024 and follows a recent 'blood moon' lunar eclipse from March 13-14, 2025, underscoring the cyclical nature of celestial events observed for millennia. Much like the 1919 solar eclipse that played a pivotal role in confirming Einstein’s theory of relativity, this deep partial eclipse is a modern reminder of our longstanding engagement with cosmic alignments. Over the next century, while the physical phenomena themselves are predictable, their cultural and scientific impacts continue to evolve, reflecting our enduring curiosity and the value of careful, communal skywatching.
