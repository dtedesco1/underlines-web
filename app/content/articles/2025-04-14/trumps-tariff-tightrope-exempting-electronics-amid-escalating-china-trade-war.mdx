# Trump’s Tariff Tightrope: Exempting Electronics Amid Escalating China Trade War

In mid-April 2025, Trump's administration exempted smartphones, computers, and other key electronics from its steep 125% tariffs on Chinese goods while warning of upcoming separate levies on semiconductors and select products.

#### Focusing Facts

- On April 12, US Customs listed 20 electronics categories, including smartphones and computers, as exempt from reciprocal tariffs.

- Commerce Secretary Lutnick announced that targeted tariffs on semiconductors and pharmaceuticals will be implemented within two months.

- China retaliated by raising its tariffs on US imports to 125%, intensifying trade tensions.

#### Context

This maneuver echoes past protectionist measures such as the 1930 Smoot-Hawley Tariff, yet differs by delineating exceptions to mitigate domestic economic disruption while pushing for US manufacturing on strategic items. The selective exemption highlights modern trade policy’s balancing act between national security concerns and economic pragmatism, reflecting structural shifts in global supply chains over recent decades. On a century-long scale, while these adjustments may appear tactical, they signal a broader realignment in the US-China economic rivalry and the future of high-tech production.
