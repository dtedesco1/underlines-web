# EU Unveils €800 Billion 'Rearm Europe' Initiative Amid US Aid Freeze

On March 4, 2025, EU Commission President Ursula von der Leyen introduced a multibillion-euro defense mobilization plan to rapidly boost European military spending over the next decade in direct response to the U.S. halting its military aid to Ukraine.

#### Focusing Facts

- The proposal aims to mobilize nearly €800 billion (around $840 billion) in defense spending, including €150 billion in new joint EU borrowing.

- EU member states are urged to raise defense budgets by 1.5–2.5% of GDP, potentially unlocking up to €650 billion in fiscal space over four years.

- The plan was catalyzed by U.S. President Trump's suspension of aid to Ukraine and accompanying calls for European security self-reliance.

#### Context

This initiative marks a significant pivot in European security policy, echoing historical shifts like the Cold War era’s drive for national defense capabilities and the 1990s moves towards deeper European integration. While Europe has long depended on American military support—a dynamic that has historically shaped its defense posture—the 2025 proposal reflects a broader trend towards strategic autonomy amid evolving geopolitical pressures. In the wider sweep of history, this mirrors transformations seen after the World Wars and during the Cold War, where alliances were redefined in response to shifting power balances. Such rearmament efforts promise long-term impacts on the balance of power in Europe, potentially influencing defense and fiscal policy debates for the next century.
