# Trump Tariff Hike Sparks Multi-National Retaliation in Trade War Escalation

On March 4, 2025, President Trump announced significant tariff increases on imports from China, Canada, and Mexico, prompting immediate countermeasures from these nations.

#### Focusing Facts

- Trump doubled tariffs on Chinese imports by adding an extra 10 percentage points, raising some duties to 45% and imposing blanket 25% tariffs on goods from Canada and Mexico.

- China responded by levying additional 10%-15% tariffs on targeted U.S. agricultural and food products and blacklisting 15-25 U.S. companies from Chinese markets.

- Canada announced an immediate $30 billion counter-tariff on U.S. products, set to escalate to $155 billion within 21 days.

#### Context

This escalation echoes past tariff confrontations such as the Smoot-Hawley Tariff Act of 1930, which, like today's measures, deepened trade disputes and economic uncertainty.
