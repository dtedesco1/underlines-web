# Brazil Indicts Bolsonaro Over Coup Plot Amid Transatlantic Legal Showdown

On February 19, 2025, Brazil charged former President Jair Bolsonaro and 33 co-defendants with orchestrating a broad conspiracy to overturn the 2022 election results, with allegations including coup plotting and assassination schemes, while U.S. entities simultaneously escalate legal actions against a Brazilian Supreme Court judge.

#### Focusing Facts

- Bolsonaro and 33 associates were indicted on February 19, 2025 by Attorney General Paulo Gonet Branco.

- Charges encompass conspiracy to subvert electoral outcomes, armed criminal association, and plans to assassinate political figures including President Lula da Silva and Justice Alexandre de Moraes.

- Key figures implicated include former minister Walter Braga Netto and other close allies from Bolsonaro’s administration.

#### Context

This case marks a significant institutional response reminiscent of Brazil’s own post-military dictatorship consolidation in the mid-1980s, when judicial and democratic forces reasserted control to prevent authoritarian revival. As Bolsonaro’s alleged plot parallels historic challenges—such as the polarized struggles during the Weimar era and Latin America's turbulent transitions in the late 20th century—these legal moves reinforce long-term trends where independent judiciaries serve as bulwarks against anti-democratic shifts. On a centennial scale, this episode may be seen as a watershed moment that underscores the resilience of democratic institutions in the face of populist and conspiratorial threats, setting a precedent that contrasts sharply with less consequential reckonings in other democracies.
