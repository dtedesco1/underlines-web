# Global Glacier Melt Accelerates: 273 Billion Tonnes Lost Annually

A comprehensive international study published on February 19, 2025 in Nature reveals that glaciers worldwide are melting at an accelerating pace, losing an average of 273 billion tonnes of ice per year—a 36% increase in the rate of loss between 2012 and 2023.

#### Focusing Facts

- Global glaciers have lost around 5% of their total volume since 2000.

- Regional losses hit 39-40% in the European Alps and Pyrenees, contributing 18mm of sea level rise.

- The ice loss equals 30 years of global water consumption and roughly three Olympic swimming pools’ worth of ice melting every second.

#### Context

This event echoes the historic shifts first noted in mid-20th century glacier studies, such as those in the 1980s that began documenting retreat in the Alps, but the current pace far outstrips earlier observations. The integration of diverse satellite and field data shows a systemic acceleration in ice loss, reflecting long-term trends of industrial-era warming. Over a century, this consistent and accelerating melt not only alters freshwater availability and mountain landscapes but also portends significant impacts on sea levels and coastal communities, challenging infrastructure and water security in an era when climate change is a pivotal driver of environmental transformation.
