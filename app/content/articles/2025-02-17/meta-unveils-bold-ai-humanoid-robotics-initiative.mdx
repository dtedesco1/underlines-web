# Meta Unveils Bold AI Humanoid Robotics Initiative

On February 14, 2025, Meta Platforms announced the creation of a dedicated team within its Reality Labs division to develop AI-powered humanoid robots focused on household tasks and a broader platform for third-party robotics.

#### Focusing Facts

- The new team, led by Marc Whitten (ex-Cruise CEO), was revealed in an internal memo by CTO Andrew Bosworth.

- Meta plans to invest $65 billion in AI, robotics, and AR/VR products during 2025.

- The initiative includes integrating Meta's Llama AI platform and discussions with partners like Unitree Robotics and Figure AI.

#### Context

Historically, tech giants have repeatedly shifted paradigms by leveraging existing strengths – much like Ford’s assembly line in 1913 revolutionized production by streamlining complex processes, and similarly, the smartphone ecosystem was transformed by platforms like Android in the late 2000s. Meta’s move into humanoid robotics is not just a response to current competition from Tesla and the budding interests of Apple, but also a reflection of long-term trends integrating digital intelligence with physical automation. Over a century, such technological pivots have redefined industries and labor markets, making this initiative a potentially foundational step in the ongoing evolution of human-machine interaction.
