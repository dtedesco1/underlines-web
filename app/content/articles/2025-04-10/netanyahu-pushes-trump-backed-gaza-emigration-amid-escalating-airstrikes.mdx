# Netanyahu Pushes Trump-Backed Gaza Emigration Amid Escalating Airstrikes

On April 2025, Israeli Prime Minister Netanyahu doubled down on a controversial plan endorsed by President Trump to forcibly relocate Gaza’s 2 million residents, even as renewed airstrikes intensified the humanitarian crisis.

#### Focusing Facts

- Trump proposed transferring Gaza’s 2 million citizens to create a ‘Gaza Riviera’, with discussions involving potential host nations.

- In March 2025, Israel established a dedicated administrative body to facilitate 'voluntary' migration from Gaza, signalling operational steps toward the plan.

- Recent Israeli airstrikes have killed 23 or more civilians in single incidents, with over 50,000 Palestinian deaths reported since hostilities resumed.

#### Context

This episode reflects a chilling echo of past forced displacement policies, notably the 1948 Nakba and post‐World War II population transfers. While critics warn that adopting measures reminiscent of ethnic cleansing could further destabilize the volatile Middle East, proponents claim it is a strategic response to decades-long security and territorial dilemmas. The blending of military action with demographic engineering marks a dangerous precedent that challenges international law and humanitarian norms, portending long-term regional and global ramifications over the next century.
