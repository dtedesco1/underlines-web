# China Escalates to 84% Tariff Amid U.S. 104% Chinese Import Levies

On April 9, 2025, China drastically increased its tariffs on U.S. goods to 84% in direct retaliation to Trump's sweeping 104% tariff on Chinese imports, further intensifying the already volatile trade war.

#### Focusing Facts

- China raised its tariff rate from an initial 34% to 84% after U.S. tariffs on Chinese goods hit 104% following an added 50% levy.

- The U.S. simultaneously paused new higher tariffs for 90 days on most countries while escalating measures against China—later raising tariffs on Chinese exports to 125%.

- Global markets reacted sharply, with U.S. and Asian stock indices plunging and currency values fluctuating amid fears of a deepening global recession.

#### Context

This latest escalation mirrors historical tariff confrontations such as the 1930 Smoot-Hawley Tariff, which contributed to global economic decline, and reflects a continuation of the US-China trade war initiated in 2018. Unlike the more contained disputes of the past, today’s conflict occurs in an era of intricate global supply chains and rapid information flows, making economic repercussions widespread. Over the long term, these tit-for-tat measures highlight systemic shifts toward protectionism that could permanently reorient global economic ties rather than representing a transient policy spat. This moment, therefore, may contribute to entrenched geopolitical and economic realignments beyond mere electoral or policy cycles.
