# Musk Unveils Mars Mission: Starship to Launch Tesla’s Optimus by End-2026

Elon Musk announced that SpaceX’s reusable Starship, carrying Tesla’s humanoid Optimus robot, is set to depart for Mars at the end of 2026, with potential human landings as early as 2029 or more likely in 2031.

#### Focusing Facts

- Starship, at 123 meters tall, is the world’s largest and most powerful launch vehicle.

- Recent test flights have experienced multiple failures, including explosions shortly after liftoff, prompting FAA investigations.

- The mission also marks Tesla's effort to deploy its Optimus robot—a technology showcased in 2022 with an anticipated retail price of $20,000–$30,000—as a precursor to future human colonization.

#### Context

This announcement builds on decades of audacious space ambitions, evoking historical milestones like the Apollo program's Saturn V launches in the 1960s, yet targets a commercial future where private-sector innovation takes center stage. While past explorations prioritized governmental prestige and national security, Musk’s plan reflects a long-term vision where iterative testing and rapid innovation could reshape interplanetary travel. However, technical setbacks and regulatory challenges—reminiscent of past experimental hazards—suggest that achieving reliable, safe deep-space flight is still uncertain. Over the next century, if these risks are managed, such missions may fundamentally alter human civilization's relationship with space by establishing a multi-planetary presence.
