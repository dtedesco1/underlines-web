# SpaceX Crew-10 Launch Enables Rescue of Extended Starliner Astronauts

On March 14, 2025, SpaceX launched the Crew-10 mission to relieve ISS astronauts stranded due to Boeing Starliner malfunctions, paving the way for their long-delayed return.

#### Focusing Facts

- A SpaceX Falcon 9 rocket lifted off at 7:03 p.m. ET from Kennedy Space Center after a previous delay over a hydraulic issue.

- NASA astronauts Butch Wilmore and Suni Williams, originally slated for a week-long mission via Starliner, have been orbiting since June 2024, now extended to nine months.

- The Crew-10 crew includes Anne McClain, Nichole Ayers, Japan’s Takuya Onishi, and Russia’s Kirill Peskov, marking a multinational team on a six-month ISS rotation.

#### Context

This mission echoes the demanding schedule adjustments seen during the Apollo era—where unforeseen technical setbacks (such as Apollo 13's crisis in 1970) necessitated rapid and innovative mission revisions. Over decades, evolving spacecraft design and launch strategies have repeatedly demonstrated that bridging technological and procedural gaps is critical to mission success. In the context of a 100-year spaceflight evolution, the balance of technical risk management and political oversight remains as pivotal as ever.
