# Asteroid 2024 YR4: Global Tracking as 2% Impact Risk Emerges for 2032

Multiple observatories and space agencies are intensifying efforts to track asteroid 2024 YR4, a 40–100 meter rock with a roughly 2% chance of colliding with Earth in 2032, prompting renewed planetary defense initiatives.

#### Focusing Facts

- Discovered on December 27, 2024 by the ATLAS telescope, the asteroid's impact odds have been computed between 1.4% and 2.3% by various agencies.

- Gemini South captured detailed images on Feb 7, 2025 and the James Webb Space Telescope is slated to begin infrared observations in March to extend its trajectory data.

- The asteroid approached at 828,800 km on Christmas 2024 and, if a hit occurs, could produce a blast comparable to regional historical events like Tunguska (1908) and Chelyabinsk (2013).

#### Context

This renewed focus on 2024 YR4 reflects a century-long evolution in asteroid monitoring—from the devastating Tunguska event in 1908, which highlighted Earth's vulnerability, to modern demonstrations like the 2022 DART mission. The evolving global network that now includes NASA, ESA, China’s emerging planetary defense force, and private observatories underscores an enduring trend of increased vigilance and international cooperation in space security. While the current odds remain low, the coordinated, precision-driven approach of modern agencies marks a critical shift in our long-term ability to understand and potentially deflect near-Earth objects, shaping a future where such cosmic threats are managed with ever-improving technology and preparedness.
