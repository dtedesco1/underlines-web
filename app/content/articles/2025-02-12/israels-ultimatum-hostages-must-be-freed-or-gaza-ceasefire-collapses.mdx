# Israel’s Ultimatum: Hostages Must Be Freed or Gaza Ceasefire Collapses

On February 11, 2025, Prime Minister Netanyahu and President Trump issued a stark ultimatum to Hamas: release all hostages by Saturday noon or face the end of the ceasefire and a resumption of intense combat.

#### Focusing Facts

- The ceasefire, initiated after the October 2023 incident, stipulated the release of 33 hostages in exchange for nearly 2,000 Palestinian prisoners, with ongoing rounds of swaps.

- Both Israeli and US leaders set a hard deadline of Saturday at 12 p.m. (Feb. 15), warning that failure to meet it would trigger renewed military operations and collapse negotiations.

- Yemen's Houthis have declared readiness to attack Israel should hostilities resume, adding another layer to the region’s instability.

#### Context

This event is reminiscent of past geopolitical flashpoints, such as the heightened tensions of the 1973 Yom Kippur War and the hostage exchange dilemmas post-1972 Munich Olympics. The ultimatum reflects longstanding patterns where ceasefire agreements in the Middle East are precarious and liable to unravel under domestic and international pressures. The hostage crisis and its severe deadline exemplify the complex interplay between military posturing and diplomatic negotiations, undercutting any long-term strategic stability and echoing over a century of conflict where temporary truces fail to resolve deep-rooted issues. In a broader context, while immediate threats of renewed combat stir regional apprehension, the structural challenges of achieving a lasting peace in the Middle East persist, marking this as another episode in a long historical series of ephemeral deals and rapid escalations.
