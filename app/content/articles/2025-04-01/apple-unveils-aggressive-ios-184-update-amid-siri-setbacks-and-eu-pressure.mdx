# Apple Unveils Aggressive iOS 18.4 Update Amid Siri Setbacks and EU Pressure

Apple accelerates its iOS 18.4 rollout with advanced Apple Intelligence features while internal delays in the Siri overhaul and new European regulations pressure its walled ecosystem.

#### Focusing Facts

- iOS 18.4 release candidate launched on March 28, targeting an early April debut with features like Priority Notifications and a new Sketch mode for Image Playground.

- Apple confirmed delays in the promised Siri overhaul, shifting leadership and hinting at a more comprehensive Siri 2 update in iOS 19.

- The European Commission mandated interoperability measures under the DMA to open up Apple's ecosystem to third-party accessories.

#### Context

This development encapsulates a recurring theme in Apple’s long history of bold innovations coupled with operational challenges. Much like the disruptive launch of the iPhone in 2007, iOS 18.4 is designed to reshape user interaction through AI-driven features across its product lineup. However, the current scenario is complicated by the company’s unmet promises regarding Siri’s upgrade and the external pressure to relinquish aspects of its traditionally closed ecosystem—a situation reminiscent of past industry battles over standardization and openness. While Apple’s aggressive integration of Apple Intelligence marks an evolution in its software strategy, the delayed Siri enhancements and regulatory interventions hint at broader systemic challenges. In the coming decades, these issues may serve as pivotal moments that recalibrate expectations around innovation, market control, and open competition in the tech sector.
