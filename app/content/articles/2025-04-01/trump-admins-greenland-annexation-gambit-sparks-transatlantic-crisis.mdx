# Trump Admin’s Greenland Annexation Gambit Sparks Transatlantic Crisis

Between March 30-31, 2025, President Trump's administration escalated its territorial ambitions by asserting that the US will annex Greenland without ruling out military force, triggering sharp rebukes from Denmark and Greenland’s new leadership.

#### Focusing Facts

- Trump declared on NBC, 'We'll get Greenland. Yeah, 100%,' while not dismissing the option of military intervention.

- Vice President JD Vance visited Pituffik Space Base on March 31, 2025, after diplomatic protests derailed a broader itinerary with Greenland officials.

- Greenland’s newly sworn-in PM Jens-Frederik Nielsen unequivocally stated, 'We do not belong to anyone else,' rejecting US claims.

#### Context

This incident echoes historical episodes such as the 1951 US-Denmark defense agreement, where post-WWII arrangements secured Arctic security through cooperation rather than annexation. Today’s aggressive tone harks back to 19th-century expansionist maneuvers like Manifest Destiny, yet clashes with modern norms of self-determination and alliance fidelity. Over the long haul, such expansionist posturing risks undermining established diplomatic frameworks and shifting great power dynamics in the Arctic, where rising claims by Russia and China further complicate a century-long trend toward multilateral security and treaty-based order.

#### Narrow Perspectives

- **Pro-expansionist national security advocates**: This group sees the pursuit of Greenland as a necessary strategic move to secure U.S. interests and counter rival powers, emphasizing that Denmark has underinvested in Arctic security. They tend to downplay the violation of international norms and indigenous self-determination in favor of a strong nationalist security agenda. ([Yahoo](https://www.yahoo.com/news/trump-says-u-greenland-military-005340629.html), [POLITICO](https://www.politico.eu/article/usa-donald-trump-military-intervention-greenland-again-denmark/))

- **Anti-imperialist diplomatic critics**: This group condemns the U.S. attempt to annex Greenland as a reckless, imperialist maneuver that disrespects the island's self-determination and undermines longstanding alliances. They may overly emphasize moral and diplomatic shortcomings, potentially ignoring legitimate security concerns in their critique of U.S. foreign policy.
