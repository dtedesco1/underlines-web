# Microsoft's Faulty Update Triggers Global Service Outage

On March 1, 2025, a defective service update caused widespread disruptions across Microsoft’s key platforms, affecting tens of thousands between 3:30 p.m. and early recovery hours ET.

#### Focusing Facts

- Over 37,000 complaints about Outlook were logged on Downdetector starting around 3:30 p.m. ET.

- Microsoft identified a suspected code update as the cause and reverted it, with most services recovering by early Sunday morning.

- The outage impacted multiple services including Outlook, Microsoft 365, Teams, Exchange, and Azure across major cities like New York, Chicago, Los Angeles, and regions in the UK and India.

#### Context

This incident echoes previous major disruptions such as the more than 24-hour outage in November 2023 and the global outages in 2023-2024, reflecting growing challenges in managing complex cloud infrastructures. Similar to the June 2024 incident where a problematic Windows update at CrowdStrike paralyzed critical services worldwide, this event underscores systemic risks in software rollouts that affect hundreds of millions. Over a century of technological evolution, such disruptions serve as reminders of the volatile balance between rapid innovation and the reliability of digital systems, with long-term implications for trust and dependency on centralized cloud services.
