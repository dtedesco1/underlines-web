# Israel Halts Gaza Aid as U.S.-Mediated Ceasefire Extension Fails

Following a 42-day ceasefire initiated on Jan 19, Hamas rejected a U.S.-brokered extension proposal, prompting Israel to cut off humanitarian aid to Gaza on March 2.

#### Focusing Facts

- The ceasefire exchange included the release of 33 Israeli hostages and 2,000 Palestinian detainees.

- U.S. envoy Steve Witkoff’s proposal to extend the first phase through Ramadan and Passover was declined by Hamas, triggering Israel’s aid suspension.

- Hamas currently holds 59 hostages, with negotiations pending on their staggered release under a phased deal.

#### Context

This impasse recalls past instances where blockades and aid control were used as negotiation levers—similar to the 1948 blockade of Jerusalem and later instances of strategic supply restrictions during conflicts. The current episode, set against the backdrop of enduring Israeli-Palestinian tensions, illustrates a long-term trend where humanitarian aid becomes a tool for political and military pressure. Over the past 100 years, from formal sieges in World War eras to modern asymmetric warfare, such tactics reflect a persistent pattern in international conflict management that will likely influence future negotiations and stability in the region.
