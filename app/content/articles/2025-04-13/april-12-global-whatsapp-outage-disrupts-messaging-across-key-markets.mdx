# April 12 Global WhatsApp Outage Disrupts Messaging Across Key Markets

On April 12, 2025, WhatsApp suffered a widespread outage that crippled users’ ability to send messages and update statuses, with disruptions noted in India, the US, UAE, Singapore, and beyond.

#### Focusing Facts

- DownDetector in India logged over 597 complaints by 5:22 PM IST, with later reports exceeding 1,700 complaints and 3,133 noted at peak times.

- Globally, around 87% of users reported messaging failures, while related issues also affected Meta-owned platforms like Facebook and Instagram.

- The outage coincided with technical glitches in India’s UPI system, affecting digital payment transactions simultaneously.

#### Context

This outage is reminiscent of past large-scale digital disruptions, such as the 2019 Facebook blackout that impacted billions and the early 2000s internet outages which exposed vulnerabilities in digital infrastructure. The incident reflects long-term trends in global digital dependency and the systemic challenges of maintaining resilient communication networks in a hyper-connected era. Over a century, such recurrent technical failures could drive significant reforms, underscoring the need for robust backup systems as technology becomes increasingly integral to economic and social functions.
