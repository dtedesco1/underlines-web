# France-UAE Forge €50bn AI Data Center Amid €109bn National AI Push

French and Emirati leaders unveiled a dual-pronged AI strategy on February 7-9, 2025, with the UAE committing up to €50bn to build a 1GW data center in France and President Macron announcing a sweeping €109bn AI investment plan.

#### Focusing Facts

- UAE's MGX fund to invest between €30bn and €50bn in a new 1GW data center in France (Feb 7, 2025).

- Macron confirmed French companies and international partners will deploy €109bn in AI projects, including a public interest fund with €400mn already pledged (Feb 9, 2025).

- Chinese startup DeepSeek released an LLM in January 2025 at a training cost of US$5.6M, shaking up global AI capital flows and market dynamics.

#### Context

This joint announcement comes at a transformative moment reminiscent of the 1957 Sputnik launch, which redefined geopolitical competition. Over the past decades, global tech leadership has oscillated between regions, and now, with sovereign investments in infrastructures like AI-dedicated data centers, France and the UAE are signaling a new era of public-private and international cooperation in technology. The event reflects long-standing trends where governments seek to reclaim strategic independence in critical technology sectors—a challenge seen in the U.S. space race and later in digital innovation hubs in Asia and Europe. On a 100-year timescale, these initiatives may not only reshape AI markets but also alter global power balances by institutionalizing new forms of economic and diplomatic capital in the high-stakes arena of artificial intelligence.
