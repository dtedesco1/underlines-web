# Zelenskyy Criticizes Trump’s Ukraine War Claims Amid Rising Diplomatic Tensions

#### Focusing Facts

- Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy criticized U.S. President Donald Trump for spreading disinformation about Ukraine, calling it harmful and aligned with Kremlin propaganda.

- Trump accused Zelenskyy of being a "dictator" for postponing elections due to martial law and claimed Ukraine started the war, echoing Russian narratives.

- A planned press conference between Zelenskyy and Trump's envoy, Keith Kellogg, was abruptly canceled at the U.S. delegation's request amidst escalating tensions.

#### Context

This diplomatic fallout reflects historical patterns where smaller nations are sidelined in negotiations between global powers. Similar to the Munich Agreement of 1938, where Czechoslovakia was excluded from decisions about its future, Ukraine faces challenges asserting its sovereignty in peace talks dominated by larger players. Trump's rhetoric and actions mark a significant shift in U.S. policy, raising questions about Washington's commitment to Ukraine and its alignment with European allies. The situation underscores the enduring tension between national sovereignty and great power politics, with potential long-term implications for transatlantic relations and global security.
