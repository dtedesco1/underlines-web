# Trump Approves Patriot Missiles for Ukraine, EU Will Cover the Cost

On 13 July 2025 President Trump ended a brief weapons freeze by pledging to ship U.S. Patriot air-defence systems to Kyiv, stating Europe will reimburse Washington in full.

#### Focusing Facts

- Announcement made 13 Jul 2025 at Joint Base Andrews; number of Patriots not yet fixed.

- Bipartisan Graham-Blumenthal sanctions bill—now with 85 Senate co-sponsors—would let Trump impose tariffs up to 500 % on countries financing Russia’s war economy.

- The Pentagon had halted deliveries of Patriot interceptors and 155 mm shells in early July but reversed the decision less than a week later at Trump’s direction.

#### Context

Great-power patrons periodically pivot from diplomacy to decisive materiel aid—Roosevelt’s March 1941 Lend-Lease to Britain or Nixon’s October 1973 airlift to Israel—when talks collapse and battlefield realities threaten wider interests.  Trump’s sudden hard turn on Putin follows that historical script and reflects two structural currents:  (1) Washington’s recurring role as the arsenal of democracies, now tempered by ‘buyer-pays’ burden-sharing that echoes 1950s Mutual Defense Assistance but with EU financing; (2) the weaponisation of trade—500 % tariffs and asset seizures—marking the deepening fragmentation of the post-1991 global market into rival sanction blocs.  Whether this moment endures depends on if Europe sustains payment and if Russia can circumvent Patriots with cheaper drones, but on a 100-year horizon it could signal the definitive end of hopes for a cooperative Russia-West security order born in 1991, accelerating a long arc toward economic decoupling and renewed bloc politics.
