# Iran Fires 10 Missiles at U.S. Al-Udeid Base in Qatar After U.S. Nuclear Site Strikes

On 23 June 2025 Tehran answered the U.S. bombing of three Iranian nuclear facilities by launching about ten ballistic missiles toward the American-run Al-Udeid Air Base in Qatar; Qatari air defenses intercepted the salvo and no casualties were reported.

#### Focusing Facts

- The Hill cited a source saying exactly 10 missiles were fired at Al-Udeid around 1 p.m. EDT on 23 June 2025.

- U.S. B-2 bombers had hit Iran’s Fordow, Natanz, and Esfahan nuclear sites two days earlier, on 21 June 2025.

- Qatar pre-emptively closed its airspace and evacuated the base that hosts roughly 10,000 U.S. troops before the attack.

#### Context

Iran’s move echoes its 8 Jan 2020 ballistic strike on Iraq’s Ayn al-Asad base—also announced in advance and designed to avoid U.S. fatalities—highlighting a recurring tit-for-tat logic since the 1979 revolution whereby Tehran signals resolve without inviting total war. On a century-scale, the episode underscores two structural trends: (1) the vulnerability of large, fixed U.S. installations built during the 1990-2011 Gulf wars to precision regional missiles, and (2) the accelerating entanglement of Israel-Iran conflict with direct U.S.-Iran confrontation, reminiscent of how localized Balkan disputes in 1912-14 drew in great powers. Whether the 2025 exchange becomes a mere punctuation mark like Khobar Towers (1996) or a tipping point toward unraveling the post-1991 U.S. security architecture in the Gulf will depend less on this bloodless volley than on whether Washington and Tehran can rewrite—rather than replay—their now-decades-old escalation script.
