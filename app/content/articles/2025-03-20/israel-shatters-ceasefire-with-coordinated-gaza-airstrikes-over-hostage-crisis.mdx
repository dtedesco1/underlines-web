# Israel Shatters Ceasefire with Coordinated Gaza Airstrikes Over Hostage Crisis

On March 18, 2025, Israel resumed intensive airstrikes on Gaza after Hamas rejected US-brokered ceasefire extensions and failed to secure the release of Israeli hostages.

#### Focusing Facts

- Israeli strikes, coordinated with US consultations, killed over 400 Palestinians in one heavy offensive reported on March 18.

- US envoys, including Adam Boehler and Steve Whitcoff, pushed proposals that Hamas rejected, deepening the crisis over hostage releases.

- Since the ceasefire began on January 19, Israel has been reported to violate its terms over 1,000 times and averaged three Palestinian deaths per day, with aid blockades worsening Gaza’s humanitarian crisis.

#### Context

This breakdown echoes historical collapses in ceasefire agreements, reminiscent of the diplomatic failures preceding the 1973 Yom Kippur War when strategic posturing over immediate gains led to renewed regional conflict. Here, long-term trends of militarized responses driven by hostage crises and unilateral actions are once again emerging amidst international appeals for negotiation. Over a century, global conflicts have repeatedly underscored how entrenched power imbalances and unmet humanitarian needs exacerbate tensions. This event not only deepens the immediate crisis in the Middle East but also signals a systemic challenge to traditional de-escalation efforts, demanding a reexamination of negotiations and military engagement strategies that have persisted throughout modern history.
