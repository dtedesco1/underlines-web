# Trump Imposes Sweeping Sanctions on the ICC Over Netanyahu Arrest Warrant

On February 6-7, 2025, President Trump signed an executive order imposing financial and visa sanctions on ICC officials in direct response to the court’s simultaneous arrest warrants for Israeli leader Netanyahu, his former defense minister, and a Hamas commander.

#### Focusing Facts

- Trump's order freezes assets and bans entry to the US for ICC staff and their families linked to investigations of US citizens and allies.

- The ICC issued arrest warrants in November 2024 for Netanyahu and Gallant, citing alleged war crimes during Israel's operations in Gaza, while also targeting a senior Hamas official.

- The sanctions coincide with Netanyahu’s visit to Washington amid legislative debates over ICC jurisdiction and a broader US strategy in the Middle East.

#### Context

This confrontation between a powerful non-member state and an international judicial body recalls past episodes like Trump’s 2020 sanctions against the ICC amid Afghanistan inquiries. It draws parallels with historical resistance to supranational legal authority—akin to the US pushback against the International Court of Justice in the 1980s. Established in 2002 post-Rwanda and Yugoslavia to tackle impunity, the ICC has long symbolized global accountability. However, Trump’s latest move underscores ongoing geopolitical tensions over sovereignty and judicial overreach, a contest that could reshape international legal norms and power balances for at least the next century.
