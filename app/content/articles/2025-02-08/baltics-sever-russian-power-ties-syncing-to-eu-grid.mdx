# Baltics Sever Russian Power Ties, Syncing to EU Grid

On February 8, 2025, Lithuania, Latvia, and Estonia disconnected their electricity systems from Russia's grid, marking a decisive shift after decades of reliance to integrate with the EU for enhanced security.

#### Focusing Facts

- Lithuania severed its connection at 07:43 local time, with Latvia and Estonia scheduled to follow by 0700 GMT.

- Following 24 hours of isolated operation, the Baltic grids will synchronize with the EU system via Poland at 1200 GMT on Sunday.

- The move cuts off the Kaliningrad exclave from Moscow’s main grid and follows nearly €1.6 billion in grid upgrades since 2018.

#### Context

Historically, transitions in energy infrastructure often mark broader shifts in geopolitical alignment. For instance, after the dissolution of the Soviet Union in the early 1990s, former republics reformed their power networks to forge new identities and secure independent energy supplies. Today’s Baltic decoupling, accelerated by Russia's actions in Crimea (2014) and Ukraine (2022), reflects long-standing trends toward regional autonomy and diminishing strategic vulnerabilities. Over a century, shifts like this have redefined national security frameworks, with lasting implications on economic stability and geopolitical leverage in a rapidly evolving global landscape.
