# Fifth Hostage Swap Amid Stark Humanitarian Dispute in Gaza Ceasefire

On Feb 7-8, 2025, Hamas named three Israeli hostages—Eli Sharabi, Or Levy, and Ohad Ben Ami—to be released in a high-stakes exchange amid accusations that Israel is delaying critical humanitarian aid deliveries as part of the ceasefire.

#### Focusing Facts

- Three Israeli hostages to be exchanged for 183 Palestinian detainees under a 42-day ceasefire agreement.

- Only 8,500 out of 12,000 agreed aid trucks have entered Gaza since Jan 19, with allegations of essential supplies being withheld.

- The ceasefire agreement also calls for 60,000 mobile housing units and 200,000 tents, with only 10% of tents delivered so far.

#### Context

This event draws a parallel to historical ceasefire miscommunications such as during the 1973 Yom Kippur War, and hostage negotiations like the Iranian Hostage Crisis (1979-1981), where tactical delays exacerbated trust issues. Amid entrenched regional dynamics, the current stall over humanitarian aid highlights the enduring conflict’s systemic challenges and the difficulty of reconciling military objectives with civilian needs. Over a century, such instances underscore a recurring pattern of high-stakes bargaining that, despite seeming momentous, may only represent another chapter in persistent instability rather than a definitive turning point.
