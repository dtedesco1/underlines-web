# Trump Proposes Rare Earths for Aid Deal with Ukraine

President Donald Trump announced a proposal for Ukraine to supply the US with rare earth elements in exchange for continued military assistance, amidst ongoing challenges in the Russia-Ukraine conflict.

#### Focusing Facts

- Trump specified that the US seeks rare earth elements from Ukraine as a condition for its near $300 billion support to the country.

- Ukraine's proposal to supply rare earth resources was initially part of Zelenskyy's 'victory plan' to incentivize continuous US aid.

- Russia controls significant rare earth deposits following territorial advances in Eastern Ukraine.

#### Context

This development mirrors past geopolitical maneuvers where resource-rich nations have leveraged their assets for aid, such as Afghanistan's mineral contracts post-NATO interventions. Long-term, this signals a potential shift towards transactional diplomacy where military support is directly tied to resource acquisition, echoing historical colonial resource extraction practices, albeit under a guise of diplomacy. On a century timescale, this move highlights the volatility of rare earth dependency in global power dynamics, as countries scramble to secure resources crucial for technological and military advancements. The broader question is whether this sets a precedent for future aid negotiations, potentially eroding the ethical considerations traditionally associated with humanitarian and military assistance.
