# Trump Reinstates Canada-Mexico Tariffs and Doubles China Levy for March 4

On February 27, 2025, President Trump confirmed that 25% tariffs on Canadian and Mexican imports will resume on March 4 alongside an additional 10% tariff on Chinese goods, reversing a one-month pause in the wake of promised border security measures.

#### Focusing Facts

- 25% tariffs on Canada and Mexico, paused since Feb 3, are set to take effect on March 4.

- China will face a total tariff rate of 20% as an extra 10% tariff is added to the existing 10%.

- Reciprocal tariffs on other trading partners are scheduled to begin on April 2.

#### Context

This move recalls historical episodes of protectionism, such as the Smoot-Hawley Tariff of 1930 that escalated trade tensions and market volatility. Unlike that era, today's global economy is more interconnected, yet the insistence on aggressive tariffs reflects a long-running trend of reverting to unilateral trade policy measures that can unsettle supply chains and trigger retaliatory moves. Over a century, while such actions rarely redefine the global economic order, they periodically force a reevaluation of trade partnerships and expose the risk of policy unpredictability when political pressures overwhelm data-driven decisions.
