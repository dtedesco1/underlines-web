# IDF Admits Fatal Missteps on Oct. 7, Sets Stage for Massive Gaza Offensive

Following the catastrophic October 7, 2023 Hamas attack that exposed deep-seated intelligence failures and misjudgments, Israel is now planning a sweeping, troop-heavy ground push in Gaza.

#### Focusing Facts

- On October 7, 2023, approximately 5,000 Hamas fighters infiltrated Israel in three waves, resulting in 1,200+ deaths and 251 hostages.

- Internal investigations revealed that over a decade, intelligence dismissed three near-invasions (April 2022, October 2022, April 2023) and misinterpreted dissenting views about Hamas’s capability.

- Israeli planners now aim to deploy over 50,000 troops in a coordinated Gaza assault expected to be finalized within six months.

#### Context

Much like the intelligence failures preceding the 1973 Yom Kippur War—when systemic miscalculations led to a surprise coalition attack—this event marks a pivotal moment in Israel’s military history. The fallout from October 7 exposed entrenched overconfidence, siloed thinking within intelligence circles, and a long-term trend of underestimating adversaries. Now, the shift toward an aggressive, technology-rebalanced ground campaign in Gaza reflects an institutional attempt to learn from past missteps. Over the next century, historians may view this period as a recalibration where military doctrine, intelligence reform, and the balance of regional power were irrevocably altered, echoing the transformative impacts seen in earlier mid-20th century conflicts.
