# AWS Unveils Ocelot Chip Cutting Quantum Error Correction Costs by 90%

On February 27, 2025, Amazon Web Services announced its Ocelot quantum chip prototype that leverages cat qubits to dramatically lower hardware overheads for error correction, potentially accelerating practical quantum computing by up to five years.

#### Focusing Facts

- Ocelot integrates 9 qubits (5 cat qubits and 4 transmons) on a 1 cm² chip, reducing error correction resource needs by nearly 90%.

- Developed in collaboration with Caltech and detailed in a Nature paper, Ocelot requires just 100,000 physical qubits for practical computers versus the conventional estimate of one million.

- AWS claims the new chip method could slash quantum error correction costs to one-fifth of current approaches.

#### Context

This breakthrough mirrors historical leaps in technology where hardware innovations redefine progress—comparable to the integration of transistors in the 1950s that spawned modern computing. In the 1990s, quantum error correction was a cost-prohibitive barrier, much like early mainframe limitations; now, by embedding error resilience directly into the chip’s design, AWS echoes eras such as the microprocessor revolution of the late 20th century. This moment, while still an early prototype, reflects a long-term trend toward scalable, fault-tolerant quantum machines that could fundamentally alter computational capabilities over the next century.
