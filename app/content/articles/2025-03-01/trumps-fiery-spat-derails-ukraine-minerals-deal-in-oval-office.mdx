# Trump’s Fiery Spat Derails Ukraine Minerals Deal in Oval Office

On February 28, 2025, a heated exchange in the White House between President Trump, VP Vance, and Ukrainian President Zelenskyy halted the signing of a key minerals agreement intended to bolster peace talks in the Ukraine conflict.

#### Focusing Facts

- Zelenskyy was set to sign a minerals deal linked to $175 billion in US aid, exchanging Ukraine’s natural resources for US support.

- During the meeting, Trump declared Zelenskyy ungrateful and 'not ready for peace,' accusing him of leveraging US involvement for undue advantage.

- The confrontation led to an abrupt cancellation of a scheduled press conference and raised international concerns over US commitment to Ukraine.

#### Context

This incident echoes past moments of public diplomatic strife such as the Nixon-era shakeups in foreign relations during the Vietnam War era, when political theatrics undermined stable alliance-building. Here, the clash symbolizes a deeper trend of transactional foreign policy where resource extraction is linked to strategic security promises. Over the next century, events like this could be seen as part of a broader shift from traditional diplomacy to a more unilateral, media-driven approach that may reshape longstanding alliances amid global power transitions.
