# Major Excavation Unveils Largest Private Bathhouse in Pompeii

Archaeologists unearthed a 'once-in-a-century' luxurious private bathhouse in Pompeii, potentially the largest ever found, after 2,000 years buried in volcanic ash.

#### Focusing Facts

- The bathhouse includes hot, warm, and cold rooms, exquisite artwork, and a plunge pool for 20-30 people.

- Two skeletons found in the house were identified during this excavation, belonging to a woman aged 35-50 and a young man in his teens or early 20s.

- The excavation revealed a juxtaposition between the opulent life of the wealthy and the harsh existence of slaves working in the furnace room.

#### Context

Historically, the discovery is akin to the uncovering of the House of the Faun in 1830, a significant find of elite Roman life. The trend of rediscovering Pompeii reveals ongoing interests in uncovering Roman society's dichotomies: luxury of the affluent and the plight of the enslaved. This moment is pivotal for archaeology as it coincides with advanced technologies and methodologies offering more comprehensive reconstructions of the past. It also reflects a broader historical inquiry into class disparity, heightened by similar focus in current societal discourse. This discovery, mostly untouched by prior excavations, enriches understanding of Roman social strata and domestic architecture.
