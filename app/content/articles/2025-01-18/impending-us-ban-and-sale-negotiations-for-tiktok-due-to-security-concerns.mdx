# Impending US Ban and Sale Negotiations for TikTok Due to Security Concerns

TikTok faces a potential US ban by January 19 unless it divests from ByteDance, amid national security concerns.

#### Focusing Facts

- Wedbush analyst Dan Ives estimates TikTok's value at $100 billion-$200 billion with its algorithm and $40 billion-$50 billion without it.

- Prominent figures like Frank McCourt and Steven Mnuchin have expressed interest in purchasing TikTok.

- President-elect Trump has sought a delay on the TikTok ban to negotiate a solution.

#### Context

This situation echoes the 2020 attempted divestiture of TikTok under the Trump administration, highlighting ongoing geopolitical tensions between the US and China over technology and data security. The current events reflect an increasing global trend towards digital sovereignty, where nations prioritize control over digital infrastructure to secure national interests. The outcome of this situation could redefine international norms regarding app ownership and data control, marking a pivotal moment in global digital policy. It reflects a broader shift towards protecting domestic technology ecosystems while anticipating repercussions on international business relations.

- **U.S. Officials and Political Contributors**: U.S. officials express concerns that TikTok's proprietary algorithm could be manipulated by Chinese authorities, justifying calls for a potential ban on national security grounds.
