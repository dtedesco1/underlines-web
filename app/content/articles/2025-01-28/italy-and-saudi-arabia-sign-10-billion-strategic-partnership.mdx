# Italy and Saudi Arabia Sign $10 Billion Strategic Partnership

Italian Prime Minister Giorgia Meloni and Saudi Crown Prince Mohammed bin Salman signed a $10 billion agreement to establish a Strategic Partnership Council aimed at enhancing cooperation across multiple sectors.

#### Focusing Facts

- The total value of the agreements signed between Italy and Saudi Arabia during Meloni's visit amounted to approximately $10 billion.

- The visit also included the signing of a deal by Italy's credit agency Sace, securing $6.6 billion in agreements with Saudi businesses.

- Saudi Arabia is Italy's second-largest trading partner in the region, with 2023 trade totaling $10.796 billion.

#### Context

This event reflects a historical pattern of Western countries engaging in strategic partnerships with Middle Eastern nations to secure energy needs and geopolitical alliances, reminiscent of the Sykes-Picot Agreement of 1916 that divided control over the Middle East. In a broader context, the agreements align with long-term global trends of interdependence in energy and economic sectors, amidst a shifting balance of power where regional cooperation becomes critical. This moment signifies a deepening of Italy's commitment to the Middle East, a region pivotal in global trade and political stability, marking a significant development that could influence European-Middle Eastern relations well into the 21st century.
