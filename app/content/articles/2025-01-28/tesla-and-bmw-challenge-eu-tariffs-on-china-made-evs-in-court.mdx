# Tesla and BMW Challenge EU Tariffs on China-Made EVs in Court

Tesla and BMW have filed legal challenges against the EU's newly imposed tariffs on electric vehicles made in China, with Tesla receiving a 7.8% rate in contrast to others facing up to 35.3%.

#### Focusing Facts

- Tesla's lawsuit was filed in the European Court of Justice's General Court last week.

- The EU's tariffs were implemented in October 2024, following an anti-subsidy investigation.

- Chinese EVs captured a 27.2% market share in the EU by the second quarter of 2024.

#### Context

The legal battle between Tesla, BMW, and the EU over tariffs echoes historical trade conflicts, such as the US-Japan auto trade tensions of the 1980s, where protectionist measures were contested. This situation highlights ongoing globalization challenges, with countries balancing domestic protection against international trade interdependence. The EU's action reflects a broader trend of scrutinizing Chinese market practices, aligning with US policies under both Biden and Trump administrations. Over a 100-year timescale, this legal conflict could signify a pivotal moment in the global shift towards electric vehicles, affecting trade dynamics and regulatory landscapes like the 20th-century automobile industry revolution. The outcome may set legal precedents impacting future trade agreements and competitive strategies in the burgeoning EV market.
