# Global Leaders and Survivors Commemorate 80th Anniversary of Auschwitz Liberation

On January 27, 2025, survivors and dignitaries from over 50 countries gathered at Auschwitz to commemorate the 80th anniversary of its liberation, amidst reports of resurgent antisemitism.

#### Focusing Facts

- 50 survivors and representatives from 54 countries attended the ceremony.

- A recent survey shows that 46% of young adults in France are unaware of the Holocaust.

- No political leaders spoke at the ceremony, with the focus solely on survivors' testimonies.

#### Context

The 80th anniversary of Auschwitz's liberation draws parallels to efforts like Germany's post-World War II 'denazification' process, illustrating ongoing challenges in confronting past atrocities. This event highlights broader trends of rising nationalism and historical distortion reminiscent of troubling eras, such as the interwar period in Europe. In a 100-year perspective, preserving memory and understanding of the Holocaust remains critical as living witnesses fade, providing an essential check against the repeating cycles of bigotry and extremism in modern global politics.
