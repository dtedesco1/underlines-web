# Amazon’s Project Kuiper Enters Operational Phase With 27-Satellite Launch, as Starlink Records 250th Flight

On 28 April 2025 Amazon lofted its first 27 production Kuiper broadband satellites aboard a ULA Atlas V, formally starting the 3,236-satellite rollout and ending Starlink’s monopoly just hours after SpaceX flew its 250th Starlink mission.

#### Focusing Facts

- Launch KA-01 lifted off from Cape Canaveral at 7:01 p.m. EDT on 28 Apr 2025, placing 27 Kuiper spacecraft into a 450 km transfer orbit.

- SpaceX’s earlier Falcon 9 flight the same day deployed 23 Starlink craft (13 with direct-to-cell payloads), bringing Starlink’s active fleet above 7,200.

- Amazon must place 1,618 Kuiper satellites in orbit before mid-2026 to satisfy its FCC licence or secure a waiver.

#### Context

Commercial satellite swarms echo past infrastructure races: Western Union’s 1861 trans-continental telegraph or the 1998 Iridium–Globalstar duel, when speed to network density decided survival.  Today’s contest sits at the intersection of reusable rockets, cloud computing and state interest in resilient comms.  The Kuiper launch shows deep-pocketed rivals can still enter despite SpaceX’s six-year head start, challenging the winner-take-most narrative.  Long-term, a handful of private constellations could shape global data routing the way the undersea cable cartels did in the late 19th century, raising sovereignty, debris and spectrum dilemmas that treaties from 1967’s Outer Space Act never anticipated.  Whether Kuiper thrives or clutters orbit will matter for decades: LEO real estate is finite, but the appetite for always-on connectivity—and for governments to tap privately owned space nets—appears on a 100-year trajectory toward critical infrastructure status.
