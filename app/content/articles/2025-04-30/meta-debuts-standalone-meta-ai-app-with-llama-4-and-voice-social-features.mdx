# Meta Debuts Standalone 'Meta AI' App with Llama 4 and Voice-Social Features

On 29 April 2025 Meta quietly converted its Meta View glasses companion into a new independent Meta AI app, giving users direct, voice-first access to its Llama 4 assistant outside the company’s legacy social networks.

#### Focusing Facts

- The app launched 29 Apr 2025 on Apple’s iOS store and the meta.ai website, with full-duplex voice mode restricted to the US, Canada, Australia and New Zealand.

- Ray-Ban Meta smart-glasses management was folded into the new app, automatically migrating existing device pairings and data to a dedicated “Devices” tab.

- Meta claims nearly one billion people already interact with Meta AI across Facebook, Instagram, WhatsApp and Messenger integrations.

#### Context

Tech giants have rolled out personal assistants before—Apple’s Siri in 2011, Amazon’s Alexa in 2014—but those stayed siloed inside hardware or operating systems. Meta’s move echoes Facebook’s 2007 Platform gambit: turn a captive social audience into a developer and data ecosystem, this time around generative AI. By merging eyewear, web, and mobile access points under one login, Meta is tightening its feedback loop between behavioral data and model fine-tuning, a strategy reminiscent of Google’s 2016 decision to unify Android Assistant usage to train its neural nets. In the longer arc, the shift hints at a century-scale convergence where personal computing, social identity, and ambient AI co-evolve; whether this moment marks empowerment or deeper surveillance will depend on how users and regulators react to a single firm’s ability to interlink eye-level hardware, social graphs, and generative models.
